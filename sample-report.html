Key Findings

What's holding you back

Critical

No structured data on any page

None of the six pages analyzed contain schema.org markup. AI systems use structured data to understand what a business is, where it's located, what it offers, and who runs it. Without it, every AI platform treats Blue Ridge Outfitters as an anonymous webpage, not a named business entity. Adding a LocalBusiness schema block to the homepage alone would improve the Schema score from 12 to approximately 55.

Critical

Google Business Profile not claimed

A search for "Blue Ridge Outfitters Roanoke" surfaces a GBP listing with incomplete information and no owner response. Google Gemini and Google AI Overviews draw heavily from GBP data when answering local business queries. Claiming and completing this profile is the single highest-impact action available and costs nothing.

High

Homepage content is 210 words — too thin to cite

AI assistants need self-contained passages they can extract and quote. The homepage contains no question-answering content, no FAQ section, and no specific facts about products, services, or expertise. Adding a 200-word "Who we are and what we carry" block, plus 4-5 FAQ items, would significantly increase citability.

High

No llms.txt file

llms.txt is an emerging standard that tells AI crawlers what your site is about and which pages to prioritize. It takes 15 minutes to create and signals to AI systems that the site is AI-aware — a small but meaningful trust signal for newer platforms.

Medium

No off-site brand presence

AI systems build entity confidence by cross-referencing a business across multiple sources. Blue Ridge Outfitters has no LinkedIn page, no press mentions, and no verified presence on directories beyond Yelp. A complete LinkedIn company page and one local press mention would meaningfully lift the Brand Authority score.
